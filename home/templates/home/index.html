  • 09:30 am

    Arrival of Barat

    The arrival of barat is an exciting moment in an Indian wedding. The groom and his family arrive at the wedding venue, to formally begin the wedding festivities.

  • 10:00 am

    Milni & Tea

    The word "Milni" comes from the Hindi word "Milan," which means meeting or joining. This auspicious ceremony signifies the coming together of two families

  • 11:00 am

    Anand Karaj

    Anand Karaj is a sacred and joyous union between two souls who are committed to living a life of love, equality, and mutual respect. The Anand Karaj emphasizes the importance of equality and encourages the couple to work together as equal partners in their journey of life.

  • 12:30 am

    Guru Ka Langar

    The tradition of Langar was started by Guru Nanak Dev Ji, the founder of Sikhism, who believed that sharing food is a way of promoting equality and eliminating discrimination. It is not just a meal but an act of selfless service and a way to connect with others in a spirit of unity and brotherhood.

  • 15:00 am

    Doli

    The doli symbolizes the departure of the bride from her family's home and her transition into her new life with her husband. The doli ceremony is a traditional and important part of Sikh weddings and is often accompanied by music, dancing, and celebration.
